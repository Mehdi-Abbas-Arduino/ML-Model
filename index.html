GreenGuard : Mapping Global Greenhouse Gas Emissions.

Our project, GreenGuard , addresses the challenge of understanding the interplay between human-caused (anthropogenic) and natural greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. Leveraging a combination of satellite and model-based datasets, GreenGuard visualizes both sources and sinks of GHGs to help policymakers, scientists, and citizens gain a clearer picture of emissions' contributions to global warming.

Our Story

Climate change is accelerating, and understanding the sources of greenhouse gas emissions is crucial for effective action. At the NASA Space Apps Challenge, we developed GreenGuard , an interactive tool that combines satellite data and climate models to map both human-caused and natural GHG emissions.
Why We built GreenGuard to address the urgent need for clear, actionable data on greenhouse gas emissions. Understanding where emissions come from—whether human activities or natural sources—is critical for making informed decisions in the fight against climate change.
What GreenGuard maps global greenhouse gas emissions from both human activities and natural sources using satellite and model-based data. It provides real-time insights, visualizes emission hotspots, tracks changes over time, and predicts future climate scenarios, helping users understand the full impact of emissions on global warming.
How GreenGuard integrates satellite data from NASA with climate models to map emissions. It visualizes both human-caused and natural GHG sources, showing real-time concentrations and historical trends. Advanced algorithms predict future emissions, while interactive tools allow users to explore and analyze the data.

Integrated Satellite and Model-Based Datasets

 ○ Utilizes data from NASA’s OCO-2 (Orbiting Carbon Observatory 2) and other satellite missions to monitor CO2 emissions.
○ Combines satellite data with model-based datasets from global climate models (e.g., CMIP6) to predict GHG emissions across diverse regions.

Interactive Visualization Tools

○  Heat maps display GHG concentrations across regions, with customizable layers to filter by gas type (e.g., CO2, CH4).
○  Data dashboards allow users to compare emission sources and sinks, providing insights into the balance between human-caused emissions and natural absorption rates.

Open API Access

Provides an API that allows integration of GreenGuard’s data with other climate monitoring and policymaking tools, enabling seamless collaboration with other platforms or research projects.

Policy and Action Insights

○  Suggests actionable insights by correlating emission data with regional policies. Users can explore the effectiveness of current policies and predict the impact of new regulations.
○  Encourages users to simulate the impact of reducing emissions in various sectors and regions, providing a better understanding of where mitigation efforts can be most effective.

What's the story behind our product ?

The GreenGuard Story
Climate change is one of the greatest challenges facing humanity. Greenhouse gases (GHGs) like carbon dioxide and methane, released from human activities and natural systems, are driving global warming at an unprecedented rate. Yet, understanding the delicate balance between these emissions and how they affect the planet remains a complex task for policymakers, scientists, and citizens.

At the NASA Space Apps Challenge, we saw the need for a solution that brings clarity to this challenge. Our goal was to create a model that combines cutting-edge satellite data with model-based predictions to provide a comprehensive view of both anthropogenic (human-caused) and natural GHG emissions. This would empower decision-makers with the knowledge to act swiftly and efficiently in the race against climate change.

Thus, GreenGuard was born.

How GreenGuard Works
GreenGuard is an interactive tool that integrates multiple datasets from NASA’s satellites and global climate models. It doesn’t just stop at showing the amount of greenhouse gases being emitted—it goes further by revealing where they’re coming from, whether from human activities like industrial emissions, or from natural sources like wildfires and volcanic eruptions.

We wanted to create something that would enable users to visualize the full lifecycle of emissions, from source to sink. Using advanced data visualization techniques, GreenGuard provides real-time updates on GHG concentrations across the globe, allowing users to identify emission hotspots and track how they change over time.

But we also wanted to look ahead. GreenGuard uses sophisticated algorithms to model future emission scenarios. This helps policymakers understand the consequences of current actions and what needs to change to meet climate goals.

Why It Matters
Climate action requires knowledge—of where emissions are happening, how they are evolving, and which actions can make the biggest impact. GreenGuard offers an unprecedented level of detail that will help policymakers pinpoint emission sources and create data-backed strategies for reducing them. Citizens can also use GreenGuard to monitor climate progress and hold governments accountable.

Looking Ahead
As we look toward the future, GreenGuard will continue to grow, incorporating even more datasets and refining its models. Our hope is that GreenGuard becomes an indispensable tool in the fight to keep global temperatures in check. Through this project, we aim to contribute to a future where data empowers positive climate action, and where global warming is held at bay.
